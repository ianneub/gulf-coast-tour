## Why End Here

After 9 days of culture, history, and incredible food, the Emerald Coast is the perfect place to exhale. White sugar-sand beaches, emerald-green water, and charming small beach towns. This is "do nothing and feel great about it" territory.

## Key Spots

### Scenic Highway 30A
The 24-mile stretch of road connecting a string of planned beach communities, each with its own character:

- **Seaside** — the original New Urbanist town, famously the filming location of *The Truman Show*. Central square with food trucks, an amphitheater, and a bookshop (Sundog Books). Very walkable
- **Rosemary Beach** — more upscale, European-inspired architecture, excellent restaurants and shops. Compact and beautiful
- **Alys Beach** — striking white Bermudian architecture, the most visually dramatic town on 30A
- **Grayton Beach** — the oldest community on 30A, more laid-back and artsy

### Beaches

- **Grayton Beach State Park** — consistently rated among the best beaches in America. Coastal dune lakes, nature trails, stunning sand
- **Henderson Beach State Park** — in Destin, less crowded than public beaches, gorgeous emerald water, boardwalk access
- **Seaside Beach** — right below the town, easy access

### If You Want One More Activity

- **Destin Harbor Boardwalk** — waterfront restaurants and charter fishing boats
- **Eden Gardens State Park** — restored antebellum mansion and gardens on Choctawhatchee Bay. Moss-draped oaks, peaceful

## Food

- **Bud & Alley's** (Seaside) — rooftop bar with sunset views, excellent seafood. The signature spot on 30A
- **The Bay** (Santa Rosa Beach) — waterfront on Choctawhatchee Bay, wood-fired dishes, great atmosphere
- **Stinky's Fish Camp** (Santa Rosa Beach) — don't let the name fool you. Elevated Gulf seafood in a funky setting
- **Café Thirty-A** (Seagrove Beach) — upscale dining in a casual beach setting
- **George's at Alys Beach** — Mediterranean-Southern fusion, one of the best on 30A
- **Donut Hole Bakery Café** (Destin) — famous breakfast spot, get there early

## Lodging Notes

- 30A towns have a mix of boutique hotels, vacation rentals, and inns
- **The Pearl Hotel** (Rosemary Beach) — boutique, beautiful, check for destination charger
- **WaterColor Inn** — upscale resort between Seaside and Grayton Beach
- Vacation rental houses can be great for a group — more space and often better value
- Destin has more standard hotel/resort options if you prefer that

## Accessibility

- 30A towns are designed for walkability — flat, compact, well-maintained sidewalks
- Seaside, Rosemary Beach, and Alys Beach are all very walkable
- Beach access varies:
  - Henderson Beach State Park has boardwalk access to the beach
  - Seaside has a paved path to the beach pavilion
  - Some public access points are sand-only — check individual walkovers
- Most 30A restaurants are ground-floor with easy access

## Trip's End

This is where the driving tour ends. From here:

- **Fly home:** Northwest Florida Beaches International Airport (ECP) is 30 minutes east in Panama City Beach. Destin-Fort Walton Beach Airport (VPS) is 30 minutes west
- **Continue east:** If you want to keep going, Panama City, Apalachicola, and Tallahassee are all within a few hours
- **Ship the car:** Tesla can be shipped home from Destin/Fort Walton area if needed

Enjoy the sand, the water, and the view. You earned it.
