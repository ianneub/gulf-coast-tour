## Why San Antonio

The Alamo and the San Antonio Missions are some of the best-preserved Spanish colonial history in the country. The River Walk makes the whole downtown walkable, shaded, and accessible. This is the cultural detour that makes the hybrid route worth it.

## Key Stops

### The Alamo
- Free admission, right in the center of downtown
- Plan ~1 hour. It's smaller than you'd expect but the history is rich
- Go early to avoid crowds

### San Antonio Missions National Historical Park (UNESCO)
- Four missions south of downtown along the Mission Trail
- **Mission San José** ("Queen of the Missions") is the must-see — beautifully restored, impressive scale
- Drive between them (~15 min apart) or bike the Mission Trail

### River Walk
- 15-mile network of walkways along the San Antonio River, one level below street grade
- Flat, paved, shaded by trees and buildings
- Perfect for an evening stroll before or after dinner
- Runs right past the Alamo, hotels, and restaurants

### Pearl District
- Restored 1880s brewery turned food, culture, and shopping hub
- Great for a relaxed morning — farmers market on weekends
- Bakery Lorraine for pastries, Botika for Peruvian-Asian fusion

## Food

- **Mi Tierra Café y Panadería** — open 24 hours since 1941, Tex-Mex institution in Market Square
- **La Gloria** — Chef Johnny Hernandez's street food-inspired Mexican, on the Pearl
- **2M Smokehouse** — Texas BBQ with Mexican flavors, south side. Worth the drive
- **Schilo's Delicatessen** — open since 1917, German-Texas comfort food, root beer on tap

## Lodging Notes

- Stay downtown near the River Walk for walkability
- Hotel Emma (in the Pearl) is stunning but pricey — worth checking for destination chargers
- Drury Inn & Suites Riverwalk and Hotel Contessa are solid mid-range with good access

## Accessibility

- River Walk is fully paved and flat — one of the most accessible urban walks in Texas
- The Alamo ground floor is accessible; upper areas have stairs
- Missions have paved paths to main structures; some gravel in outer areas
- Pearl District is flat and compact
