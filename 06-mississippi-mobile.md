## Mississippi Gulf Coast Stop

Break up the NOLA-to-Mobile drive with a stop in coastal Mississippi. Two good options:

### Option A: Ocean Springs, MS (Recommended)
- Charming small arts town just across the bridge from Biloxi
- **Walter Anderson Museum of Art** — dedicated to the eccentric, brilliant Gulf Coast artist. Small, fascinating
- **Downtown gallery district** — walkable loop of shops and galleries
- Good lunch spot: **Vestige** (upscale Southern) or **The Greenhouse on Porter** (casual, local)
- Compact and walkable — 1–2 hours is perfect

### Option B: Biloxi, MS
- More touristy, casino-heavy, but has genuine Gulf Coast history
- **Biloxi Lighthouse** — one of the first cast-iron lighthouses in the South, survived Katrina
- **Biloxi Schooner Pier** — replica schooners, maritime museum
- Seafood on the waterfront: **Mary Mahoney's Old French House** (since 1964, beautiful courtyard)

## Mobile, AL

### Why Mobile

Mobile is deeply historic and perpetually underrated. It's the original Mardi Gras city (predating New Orleans by 15 years), has a beautifully preserved downtown, and the food scene punches above its weight.

### Key Stops

- **Historic downtown** — Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception (stunning interior), Fort Condé (reconstructed French colonial fort, free)
- **Mobile Carnival Museum** — the story of the original Mardi Gras, elaborate costume displays
- **USS Alabama Battleship Memorial Park** — WWII battleship and submarine you can tour. Impressive but has steep ship stairs and ladders — assess based on group's comfort
- **Bellingrath Gardens** — 65-acre estate garden south of town, beautiful in any season. Paved paths, accessible

### Food

- **Wintzell's Oyster House** — since 1938, the classic. Oysters every way
- **The Noble South** — Southern cuisine with local ingredients, excellent cocktails
- **Spot of Tea** — great lunch spot downtown, relaxed, in a historic building
- **Callaghan's Irish Social Club** — beloved burger joint with live music

## Lodging Notes

- Stay in downtown Mobile for walkability
- **The Battle House Renaissance** — historic hotel, great location
- **Homewood Suites Mobile Downtown** — reliable, check for destination chargers
- Mobile is affordable compared to NOLA — hotel budgets go further here

## Accessibility

- Ocean Springs is compact, flat, and walkable
- Mobile's downtown is flat with wide sidewalks
- Mobile Carnival Museum is fully accessible
- USS Alabama has steep stairs and narrow passages on the ship itself — the outdoor memorial grounds and aircraft displays are accessible
- Bellingrath Gardens has paved main paths; some garden sections are gravel
